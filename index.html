init(together)

init(together) started as a Southern California conference to support women starting their careers in computing through mentorship, peer collaboration, and immersion in industry and academia.

We are now planning to launch as a non-profit that provides resources for minorities who are interested in working in technology. .

About Us

We are developing a web tool navigate information about technology career options based on user interest, and to connect them with mentors, as well as guidance on how to learn the skills to break into the field. Using the guidance from the tool we are designing, individuals can join our collaborative learning community through Homago, and a community-in-progress that uses crowd-development to allow students to collaboratively work on coding projects. We are drawing from principles in participatory learning, Human-Computer Interaction, and Information Visualization to make exploring technology and the people in the community accessible to everyone.
